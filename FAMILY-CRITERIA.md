## Hard Filters (Non-Negotiable)

### Budget & Timeline
- **Goldilocks zone:** $700K–$1.5M (ideal sweet spot)
- **Maximum stretch:** $2.5M for exceptional property meeting near-perfect criteria
- **Timeline:** ASAP, contingent on selling current homes (Issaquah, WA + Anthem, AZ)

### Acreage & Terrain
- **Minimum viable:** 80–100 acres (tight but functional for 5 homes + horse facilities + venue separation)
- **Comfortable:** 150–200+ acres (ideal privacy buffer between private residences and public venue/AirBnB areas)
- **Priority:** **Quality of usable land > raw acreage count.** Need quality pasture, buildable ground, good drainage.
- **Terrain aesthetic:** Partially forested preferred (privacy + openness). Mix of cultivated meadow + tree coverage. Rolling hills or mountain views acceptable. Avoid: barren industrial landscape, extreme isolation, swamps.

### Water & Utilities (Critical)
- **Motorboat access:** ON-PROPERTY dock OR accessible launch/put-in within 30 min drive. **MOTORBOAT-specific** (not just lake access). This is Mom's non-negotiable recreation need.

- **Zoning:** Must explicitly allow or be flexible for: horses, livestock, agricultural use, short-term rentals (AirBnB), event venues.

### Subdivisions & Dwelling Potential
- **CRITICAL:** Property must support subdivision into 4–5 separate residential structures with independent utility meters/septics. Non-negotiable for compound living.
- **Zoning:** Must allow multi-dwelling residences or be flexible/grandfathered in.

### Income Generation (Non-Negotiable)
Property must demonstrably support:
1. **Wedding venue** (flat event space, parking 50+ cars, utilities for catering, guest facilities, horse-friendly aesthetics)
2. **AirBnB village** (2–6 seasonal/permanent themed cottages, separate from family homes)
3. **Equestrian business** (trail infrastructure, arena, grooming areas, pasture for boarding)

Zoning must permit: short-term rentals, event spaces, light agriculture, equestrian business.

## Climate & Geographic Boundaries

- **Four seasons:** Required. Seasonal inspiration for Katey's writing; tourism demand for wedding/AirBnB business; cultural variety.

- Rolling hills or mountain views (scenic backdrop)
- Avoid: industrial areas, mining sites, poor soil quality visible, flood-prone flats

## Dwelling Tier System (For Your Compound)

*Your target: **Tier 1** (🏡🏡🏡🏡)*

**Tier 1 (🏡🏡🏡🏡): 4+ separate residential homes**
- One home per family member (Katey, Jack, Camille, Mom, optionally Maggie)
- **Why ideal:** Maximum privacy + autonomy; reduces family friction in shared compound living; each person controls their own space, schedule, guests
- **Infrastructure:** 4+ utility meters, 4+ septics, shared well/water system, connected power grid
- **Zoning:** Requires multi-dwelling residential zoning or grandfathering
- **Cost:** Highest initial ($100K–$200K per additional home), but maximum livability

**Tier 2 (🏡🏡): 2–3 homes**
- 2–3 primary residences + 1–2 guest cottages
- **Why acceptable:** Works if one home is very large (e.g., Jack/Camille + guest wing) or cottages are fully independent
- **Trade-off:** Some shared bathroom/kitchen space needed; requires careful family communication
- **Zoning:** Most areas allow 2–3 homes per parcel

**Tier 3 (🏡): 1 primary home + guest structure**
- One main house (4+ bed/3+ bath for all family) + detached guest cottage
- **Why less ideal:** Cramped for 5 adults; limited autonomy; tension risk
- **Trade-off:** Cheapest option; fastest to move in; less scalability

**Tier 4 (🌾): Land only**
- Raw acreage, no existing structures
- **Why avoid:** Requires full build-out (2–3 years, $500K–$1M+); construction delays; highest risk
- **Only consider if:** Property is exceptional (perfect water, zoning, acreage, price) AND you have construction timeline flexibility

**Your minimum acceptable: Tier 2** (at least 2–3 homes with guest potential)

## Evaluation Process & Timeline

### Phase 1: Regional Property Search (Week 1–2)
- Compile 15–20 properties per region (Spokane, Asheville, 1–2 secondary regions)
- Visual filtering via Zillow/Google Street View (landscape appeal, existing structures, water access obvious?)
- Quick price/acreage/beds/baths pass
- **Output:** Top 8–10 properties across regions identified

### Phase 2: Detailed Screening (Week 3–4)
- Zillow deep-dive + county records review (zoning, tax info, previous sales)
- Zoning verification (multi-dwelling allowed? STR allowed? Event venue allowed?)
- Agent calls on finalists (water rights, existing utilities, building history, pricing rationale)
- **Output:** Top 3–5 properties narrowed by region

### Phase 3: Due Diligence (Week 5–6)
- Site visits to top 2–3 (visual walkthrough, photo documentation, feel-test)
- Soil testing (crop viability, drainage, foundation-ready, well water quality)
- Water/motorboat access on-site verification
- Title search, legal review of subdivision potential
- **Output:** 1–2 finalists ranked + decision made

### Phase 4: Offer & Closing (Week 7+)
- Offer negotiations (contingent on current home sales)
- Formal inspection contingencies
- Appraisal + financing approval
- Closing

**Target timeline:** 6–8 weeks from first Zillow search to purchase offer (assuming current home sales in parallel)
